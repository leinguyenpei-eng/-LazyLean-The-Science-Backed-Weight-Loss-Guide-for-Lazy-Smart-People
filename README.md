## 🔑 Key Findings

### 1. OTC Supplements: Almost All Are Hype
- **Only one FDA-approved OTC weight-loss product exists:** Orlistat (Alli, 60mg) — blocks ~30% dietary fat absorption, ~5% body weight loss with diet. Side effects include oily/greasy stools.
- All other major OTC supplements (Garcinia cambogia, Green Tea Extract, Raspberry Ketone, CLA, Hoodia, etc.) produce **less than 1 kg of weight loss** on average vs. placebo over 12 weeks — clinically insignificant.
- The FDA issued **36+ adulteration warnings** for weight-loss supplements in a single year. Many contain undisclosed stimulants, sibutramine, or thyroid hormones.
- **NIH Office of Dietary Supplements conclusion:** "Evidence supporting dietary supplements to reduce body weight is inconclusive and unconvincing."

### 2. The Only Two OTC Tools With Meaningful Evidence
- **Psyllium husk** (7–10g/day before meals): Reduces appetite via gel formation, slows gastric emptying. Clinically validated for satiety. Cost: ~$0.10–0.20/day.
- **Glucomannan capsules** (1–3g/day): Absorbs up to 50× its weight in water. Inconsistent weight loss data but meaningful for blood glucose. Cost: ~$0.15–0.30/day. **Never use tablet form** — documented esophageal obstruction risk.

### 3. Calorie Deficit Is the Only Proven Mechanism for Fat Loss
- **7,700 kcal deficit = 1 kg fat lost** (clinical standard model)
- Diet accounts for ~70%, exercise ~30% of the deficit in most studies
- Safe rate: **0.5–1 kg/week** (500–1,000 kcal/day deficit)
- A combination of fiber + protein + walking + cutting sugary drinks can create **400–600 kcal/day deficit** at ~$1.30/day — with no prescription needed

### 6. Saxenda (Liraglutide) — Clinical Protocol Summary
- **Mechanism:** Activates GLP-1 receptors in hypothalamus (reduces appetite), slows gastric emptying ~20–30%, stabilizes blood sugar via insulin/glucagon balance
- **Titration schedule:** 0.6 mg → 1.2 mg → 1.8 mg → 2.4 mg → 3.0 mg (one new dose per week, 5 weeks total)
- **Injection:** Subcutaneous, once daily, rotate sites (abdomen/thigh/upper arm)
- **16-week rule:** If <4% weight loss at full dose by week 16, FDA guideline is to discontinue
- **Approved for:** BMI ≥30, or BMI ≥27 with comorbidity; age 12+

### 7. Vitamin & Supplement Protocol for GLP-1 Users
A meta-analysis of 480,825 GLP-1 users found deficiencies in multiple micronutrients due to reduced caloric intake:

| Supplement | Why Critical | Recommended Form |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Vitamin B12 (500–1,000 mcg/day) | GLP-1 slows absorption; deficiency causes fatigue, nerve issues | Methylcobalamin |
| Vitamin D3 (1,000–2,000 IU/day) | Most common deficiency in GLP-1 users | D3 + K2 |
| Magnesium (200–400 mg/day) | Prevents cramps, poor sleep, anxiety | Magnesium glycinate |
| Protein (1.2–2.0 g/kg/day) | Prevents muscle loss during rapid weight reduction | Whey or pea protein isolate |
| Calcium (500–1,000 mg/day) | Rapid weight loss accelerates bone density loss | Calcium citrate (split doses) |
| Zinc (8–11 mg/day) | Semaglutide studies show significant serum drops | Food-first; low-dose supplement if needed |

> **⚠ Critical absorption rule:** Saxenda slows gastric emptying and delays absorption of all oral medications. Take all supplements **at least 1–2 hours** before or after your Saxenda injection.

### 8. Weight Regain After Stopping GLP-1 Medications
- Most patients regain 60–100% of lost weight within 1–2 years of stopping
- Hunger hormones (ghrelin) return to pre-treatment levels within 2–4 weeks
- "Food noise" (intrusive thoughts about eating) returns within ~30 days
- **Conclusion:** The strategy that loses the weight must become the permanent lifestyle. There is no finish line.
