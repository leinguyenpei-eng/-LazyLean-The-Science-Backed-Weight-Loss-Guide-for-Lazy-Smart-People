The Fiber Protocol

Psyllium and glucomannan are the safest, cheapest hunger-management tools available without a prescription. Here's exactly how to use them.

Psyllium Husk
METAMUCIL · ISABGOL · PSYLLIUM POWDER
Mechanism
Forms gel in stomach, slows digestion, triggers satiety hormones
Starting dose
1–2 g with 300 ml water, once/day before largest meal
Max dose (studies)
~10 g/day in divided doses (7–10 g is typical)
When to take
20–30 min before the meal you tend to overeat most
Cost estimate
~$0.10–0.20/day — one of the cheapest options available
Safety
Generally safe. Well-tolerated at recommended doses.
Start at 2/5 max dose, increase weekly
⚠ Mix immediately and drink quickly — it thickens fast. Always take with at least 240 ml water. Space 2 hours apart from any prescription medications.
Glucomannan
KONJAC ROOT · SHIRATAKI NOODLES · KONJAC POWDER
Mechanism
Absorbs up to 50x its weight in water, fills stomach, delays emptying
Starting dose
500 mg (0.5 g) once/day before a meal — very conservative start
Max dose (studies)
~3–4 g/day, max 12 weeks per study protocols
When to take
20–30 min before eating, while sitting or standing — never lying down
Evidence quality
Inconsistent results for weight, stronger for blood glucose control
Best form
Capsule or powder (NOT tablets — risk of esophageal obstruction)
Start at absolute minimum dose
⚠ NEVER use tablet form — documented cases of esophageal obstruction. Capsule or powder only. Must drink a full glass of water immediately. If swallowing difficulty, stop immediately.
Combination Protocol

The Daily Fiber Routine

MORNING
1–2g psyllium + 300ml water, 20 min before breakfast
BEFORE LARGEST MEAL
500mg glucomannan capsule + 300ml water, 20–30 min before eating
ALL DAY
1.5–2L water minimum — fiber without water is counterproductive and risky
RULE #1
Take all prescription meds 2 hours before or after fiber — fiber reduces drug absorption

The Lazy Stack

A phased 12-week protocol for people who love eating but want results. No gym membership. No prescription drugs. No suffering required.

Week 1–2 · Foundation
Set Up Your Hunger Defense
Start psyllium: 1g before your biggest meal with a full glass of water. This is your cheapest hunger-control tool.
Replace all sugary drinks with water, plain tea, or black coffee. This alone removes 200–400 kcal/day for most people.
Don't change what you eat yet. Just observe your hunger patterns and write down when you overeat most.
Start 10-minute walks after dinner. Not for calorie burn — for blood sugar control and habit building.
Week 3–4 · Eat Volume, Not Less
Hack Your Plate Without Starving
Eat your vegetables and protein FIRST at every meal. Then eat everything else you want. You'll eat less without noticing.
Replace ONE daily meal with a protein shake (30g protein, ~300 kcal). You save 300–600 kcal and stay full for hours.
Increase psyllium to 2g, twice daily. Add glucomannan capsule (500mg) before your largest meal if hunger is still high.
Stock your fridge with volume foods: cucumber, watermelon, boiled eggs, Greek yogurt, white fish. High satiety, low calorie.
Week 5–8 · Build Momentum
Layer On Small Wins
Extend walks to 15–20 minutes after 2 meals/day. At 70kg, that's ~150 kcal burned daily — equivalent to skipping a snack.
Track calories loosely for ONE week using MyFitnessPal. Most people are shocked — not to restrict, but to understand where calories hide.
Prioritize 7–8 hours sleep. Research confirms poor sleep raises ghrelin (hunger hormone) and kills weight loss motivation.
Expected progress: 2–3 kg lost. Clothes fit differently. Keep going — metabolism adapts slowly but surely.
Week 9–12 · Consolidate
Lock In the Lifestyle
Add 2x weekly bodyweight resistance (squats, pushups, chair dips). 20 minutes. More muscle = higher resting metabolism forever.
Celebrate non-scale victories: energy, sleep quality, hunger control. These predict long-term success better than the scale number.
If stalled: don't starve — re-examine liquid calories, portion sizes of grains/oils, sleep quality. One of these is usually the culprit.
Consider seeing a doctor if BMI > 30 with health conditions. GLP-1 medications (Wegovy, Zepbound) may be appropriate — but always with medical supervision.
Key Insight from Quora Community

Why People Regain Weight

Real users on Quora and Mumsnet report the same pattern after stopping weight-loss medications: hunger returns within 2–4 weeks, food noise comes back "with a vengeance," and the lost weight returns. The conclusion from both research and lived experience is the same: the strategy that loses the weight must become the lifestyle that maintains it. There is no finish line — only a new normal.

Section 07 · Prescription Only — Educational Research

Saxenda & GLP-1 Medications: Deep Dive

Clinical research summary for capstone purposes. All prescription medications require physician evaluation, blood work, and monitoring. This section is a research synthesis — not a dosing guide.

⚕
Research disclaimer: Saxenda (liraglutide) is a Schedule Rx injectable medication. In the US, BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity is required. A physician must evaluate contraindications (thyroid cancer history, pancreatitis, pregnancy, MEN2), set the titration schedule, and monitor labs every 3–6 months. The information below is sourced from FDA prescribing information, EMA product data, and peer-reviewed literature.
Part 1

How GLP-1 Agonists Work

BRAIN
Satiety Signal
Activates GLP-1 receptors in the hypothalamus → reduces appetite and food cravings. Decreases "food noise" — intrusive thoughts about eating.
STOMACH
Slows Emptying
Delays gastric emptying by ~20–30%. Food stays in the stomach longer → you feel full much longer after a small meal.
PANCREAS
Insulin Balance
Increases insulin secretion when blood sugar is high, decreases glucagon → stabilizes blood sugar and reduces energy storage signals.
RESULT
Calorie Reduction
Users spontaneously eat 16–39% fewer calories per day with no conscious restriction. This is the mechanism that makes GLP-1s so effective.

INJECTION TECHNIQUE (FDA/EMA GUIDELINES)
📅 Timing
Once daily, at any time, with or without food. Preferable to inject at the same time each day — consistency improves adherence and reduces forgotten doses.
📍 Injection Sites
Subcutaneous (under skin) only: abdomen (2+ inches from navel), upper thigh, or back of upper arm. Rotate sites within the same region to prevent amyloid deposits at injection site.
🌡 Storage
Before first use: refrigerate 2–8°C. After opening: store below 30°C or refrigerate. Discard after 30 days. Never freeze. Keep cap on pen to protect from light.
👁 Before Each Injection
Inspect solution — must be clear and colorless. Do not use if cloudy, discolored, or contains particles. Attach a fresh needle each time. Never share pen.
⚠ 16-Week Checkpoint
If ≥4% body weight not lost by week 16 on full 3mg dose, the FDA guideline is to discontinue Saxenda — further treatment is unlikely to be effective for that patient.
Part 3 · Based on OMA/ACLM Joint Advisory 2025 + Meta-Analysis of 480,825 GLP-1 Users

Vitamin & Supplement Protocol for Saxenda Users

Because Saxenda reduces calorie intake by 16–39%, users often fall short of essential micronutrients. A meta-analysis of 480,825 GLP-1 users found deficiencies in iron, vitamin D, B12, calcium, magnesium, zinc, and vitamins A, B1, C, E, K. The longer the medication is used, the greater the deficits.

CRITICAL
Vitamin B12
Energy · Nerve function · Red blood cells
Why needed: GLP-1s slow gastric emptying, reducing B12 absorption. Deficiency causes fatigue, brain fog, nerve tingling, anemia.

Research dose: 500–1,000 mcg/day oral
Best form: Methylcobalamin (better absorbed than cyanocobalamin)
Timing: Take with a meal, separate from Saxenda injection by 2+ hours
CRITICAL
Vitamin D3
Bone · Immunity · Metabolism · Mood
Why needed: Most deficient nutrient in GLP-1 users. Vitamin D deficiency is linked to obesity itself and may reduce medication efficacy.

Research dose: 1,000–2,000 IU/day; adjust per blood test
Best form: D3 (cholecalciferol) with K2 for optimal bone absorption
Timing: With a fat-containing meal for best absorption
IMPORTANT
Magnesium
Muscle · Sleep · Nerve · Blood sugar
Why needed: Reduced food intake = lower magnesium. Deficiency causes muscle cramps, poor sleep, anxiety — common Saxenda complaints.

Research dose: 200–400 mg/day
Best form: Magnesium glycinate (gentle on GI, no laxative effect)
Timing: Evening — also improves sleep quality
IMPORTANT
Protein (Whey/Pea)
Muscle preservation · Metabolism
Why needed: OMA recommends 1.2–2.0 g protein/kg/day on GLP-1 therapy. Most users fall far short. Without adequate protein, muscle loss is significant during rapid weight reduction.

Target: ~1.5 g/kg body weight/day
Best source: Whey protein isolate (or pea protein for dairy-free)
Timing: Spread across 2–3 meals/day
IMPORTANT
Calcium + K2
Bone density during weight loss
Why needed: Rapid weight loss can accelerate bone density loss. Calcium + K2 + D3 work together to maintain bone health during the weight-loss phase.

Research dose: 500–1,000 mg calcium/day from diet + supplement
Best form: Calcium citrate (better absorbed than carbonate, especially with GI issues)
Timing: Split doses — calcium competes for absorption in large amounts
SUPPORTIVE
Zinc + Iron
Immunity · Energy · Hair health
Why needed: Semaglutide studies found significant drops in serum zinc. Iron deficiency is most common in women on GLP-1 therapy. Both cause fatigue and hair loss.

Approach: Get blood test first — don't supplement iron without confirmed deficiency
Zinc dose: 8–11 mg/day from food or low-dose supplement
⚠ Do not: Take iron and calcium simultaneously — they block each other's absorption
⚠ ABSORPTION WARNING — CRITICAL FOR SAXENDA USERS
Saxenda slows gastric emptying, which delays absorption of all oral medications and supplements. The EMA prescribing information states: oral medications may work more slowly or less effectively when taken around the same time as Saxenda. Key rule: Take all oral supplements at least 1–2 hours before or after your Saxenda injection, and confirm timing with your pharmacist for any prescription medications.

Research Verdict

For pure weight loss efficacy: Zepbound > Wegovy > Saxenda — the data is clear. Saxenda's main advantages are its longer safety track record (approved 2014), daily dosing that some patients prefer, approval for adolescents 12+, and slightly lower cost. For your capstone: Saxenda is a valid research subject but would likely be modeled as the "conservative baseline" GLP-1 compared to newer, more effective agents. The vitamin protocol is essentially the same across all three — what changes is the magnitude of caloric restriction (and thus deficiency risk).
